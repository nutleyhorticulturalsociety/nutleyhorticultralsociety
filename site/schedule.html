GENERAL RULES - COMMON TO ALL SHOWS

 

 

 

1. All persons, whether or not Members of the Society, are eligible to exhibit.

 

Only Members of the Nutley Horticultural Society are eligible for the Trophies.

 

2. The entrance fee is 20p per adult class for all three shows.

 

3. All entries must be submitted on the entry forms provided and presented to the Show Secretary in accordance with the particular rules for each Show.

 

4. The Hall will be open on Show Days for the staging of entries from 9.00am.

 

Staging of entries at the Rose show will commence at 9.30am within the designated marquee on Fords Green.

 

ALL EXHIBITS MUST BE STAGED AND EXHIBITORS LEAVE THE HALL/MARQUEE BY 11.00am WHEN

 

JUDGING WILL COMMENCE.

 

5. No person may enter more than one exhibit in any one class in the schedule.

 

6. Members of the same household may not compete against each other in any ‘home-grown’ class, e.g. fruit, vegetable and flower classes.

 

7. The definition of NOVICE shall be ‘any exhibitor who has not won a 1st, 2nd or 3rd Prize for the particular Vegetables, Fruits or Flowers mentioned under ‘Novice Classes’. Entrants to this section are also eligible to enter other sections.

 

8. Pot plants must have been in the possession of the exhibitor for not less than 6 months prior to the Show.

 

9. All exhibits must remain in the Hall or Marquee until after the prize-giving has been completed

 

10. Unless prior notice to the contrary is given to the Hon. Secretary/Show Secretary, all exhibits not removed by 4.30pm may be sold for the benefit of the Society or disposed of.

 

11. Any exhibit which does not conform to the wording of the Schedule may be disqualified at the Judge’s discretion.

 

12. In any Class irrespective of the number of exhibits, prizes will be awarded at the discretion of the Judge.

 

If, in the opinion of the Judge, an exhibit does not reach the standard expected, they will award such prize or certificate as they may consider appropriate.

 

13. In all cases the decisions of the Judges are final.

 

14. All Trophies must be returned to the Show Secretary (or any Committee Member) NOT LATER THAN ONE WEEK prior to the date of the relevant Show.

SHOWING HINTS

 

 

 

1. READ THE SCHEDULE! Check the number of exhibits required by the schedule—and double check when staging exhibits.

 

2. In assessing the merits of flowers the judge will take into account: condition, size and uniformity.

 

3. Condition: Cleanliness, freshness and presence or absence of coarseness and blemishes

 

4. Size: this is meritorious if accompanied by quality.

 

5. Uniformity: the state of being alike in size, shape and colour.

 

 

 

 

 

6. Allow ample time for staging. All exhibits should be staged in accordance with the rules and schedule as attractively as possible. In close competition points for arrangement may be the deciding factor.

 

7. Use only Narcissi foliage in Narcissi classes, with a maximum of three leaves per exhibit.

 

8. Use newspaper and/or moss to stage your exhibit as upright as possible.

 

9. Try to ensure the face of the narcissus is ‘facing’ the judge, i.e. at right angles to the stem (except in species and hybrids where a pendent flower is typical such as Narcissus triandus.)

 

10. Try to ensure the perianth is not of poor or uneven colour, ribbed, thin or hooded.

 

11. Check that segments are not too narrow to overlap for a good proportion of their length or that are neither flat nor symmetrically twisted, or that have notches, nicks or tears.

 

12. Above all, ENJOY yourself.

BASIC SHOWING HINTS

 

 

 

1. READ THE SCHEDULE! Check the number of blooms requested—and double check after you have arranged your exhibit.

 

2. Remember: condition, size and uniformity are key components in judging.

 

3. When arranging your rose(s), try to ensure they are standing upright in the vase.

 

Use newspaper and/or moss to support blooms in the vase.

 

4. Cluster Roses tend to be also called Floribunda, however due to various factors such as rose breeding and good cultivation it is possible to produce a Hybrid Tea with more than one bloom per stem. It has; therefore been decided that the sole criterion for showing roses should be: whether the exhibit meets the requirements of the class in which it is entered. In effect, this means that any type of modern garden rose may be entered in any class without risk of being declared ‘not according to schedule’. (The Horticultural Show Handbook.) Therefore please check—if your rose falls into the cluster class EVEN IF it is a Hybrid Tea, you should enter the cluster class and vice versa. If in doubt ASK.

AUTUMN SHOW—SHOWING HINTS

 

 

 

1. Read the schedule! Check the number of exhibits required by the schedule and double-check when staging exhibits.

 

2. In assessing the merits of fruit and vegetables the judge will take into account: condition, size and uniformity.

 

3. Condition: cleanliness, freshness, tenderness and presence or absence of coarseness and blemishes.

 

4. Size: this is meritorious if accompanied by quality. The size of vegetable most suitable for table use varies with the consumer.

 

5. Uniformity—the state of being alike in size, shape and colour

 

6. Allow ample time for staging. All exhibits should be staged in accordance with the rules and schedule as attractively as possible. In close competition points for arrangement may be the deciding factor.

 

7. Beetroot, Carrot, Parsnips, Turnips and Swedes should have the tops cut off leaving approximately 3” and leaf stalks should be neatly tied. Soaking beetroot overnight in saltwater can help to maintain good colour throughout.

 

8. Parsnip and long-pointed carrots—the soil or growing medium should be soaked prior to careful lifting to ensure complete retention of the root.

 

9. Runner Beans—stage a uniform exhibit. Cut from the vine with scissors and exhibit pods neatly across a plate with stalks all to one end, tails facing the other, and all one way.

 

10. Courgettes—select young tender, shapely and uniform fruits not less than 4” or greater than 6” long in any colour with flower still adhering.

 

11. Onions—avoid soft, stained specimens with thick immature necks. Avoid overskinning. Uniform, well-ripened bulbs of good colour are required. The tops should be tied or whipped using raffia, undyed string or twine. You can stage onions on rings or soft collars to keep them upright. Shallots can be staged on silver sand.

 

12. Potatoes—two classes: white or coloured and of any shape. Select medium sized specimens, generally not more than 6ozs. Freedom from skin blemishes which may be caused by pests, diseases or careless handling is essential. The tubers should be carefully washed with a soft sponge. Wiping over with milk can improve the colour and texture of skin.

 

13. Tomatoes—Select fruit of the right shape, size and colour for the cultivar. Fruit must not be overripe or with hard ‘greenback’ colouring around the calyx. Aim for a uniform set of fruit with small eye, and firm, fresh calyx.

 

14. Fruits—cut as near to the show time as practicable. Leaving stalks/stems intact and aim for a neat attractive presentation, symmetrical if possible. In ‘other’ fruit classes, lay the fruit on one or two good quality leaves.

 

15. Flowers—should be as fresh as possible, remove any damaged or discoloured flowers or foliage. Cut a portion from the base of the flower stems to assist the uptake of water. Check that all containers are filled with water. Strive to produce exhibits of good balance with flowers of even size and quality which are accommodated in containers of suitable proportions. Use newspaper of moss in containers to keep flowers upright and ‘facing’ the judge. Only use foliage if stated. If in doubt ASK!

 

16. Last but not least—Have FUN—GOOD LUCK!
